# Content Guidelines

## Nonpartisan Writing Standards

1. **Present facts, not opinions** — Report what candidates say and what ballot questions propose. Do not endorse.
2. **Attribute opinions** — "Candidate X stated…" not "Candidate X is right about…"
3. **Balanced coverage** — Give equal space to all candidates in a race. Present pro and con arguments for ballot questions.
4. **Neutral language** — Use "supports/opposes" not "fights for/attacks". Use "the proposal would" not "this good/bad bill will".
5. **Verifiable claims only** — Every factual claim must cite a source (Maine Secretary of State, city clerk, official campaign documents).

## Sourcing Rules

- **Primary sources preferred**: Maine Secretary of State (maine.gov/sos), South Portland city clerk, candidate campaign websites.
- **Secondary sources acceptable**: Portland Press Herald, Bangor Daily News — but attribute to the publication, not as fact.
- **No Wikipedia as sole source** for candidate positions.
- **Dates**: Always cite the authority (e.g., "According to the Secretary of State, the deadline is…").
- **Source summaries**: Every source cited on a page must have a non-empty `summary` field in `_data/sources.js` (1-3 sentences describing what the source covers and why it's relevant). This is the agent's responsibility — populate during content authoring.

## Style

- Use AP style for dates and names.
- Write at an 8th-grade reading level (Flesch-Kincaid).
- Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max).
- Bold key terms for scannability.

## Prohibited

- Endorsements or evaluative language ("the best candidate", "a dangerous proposal").
- Unattributed claims.
- Campaign rhetoric presented as fact.
- AI-generated "analysis" without clear source attribution.
