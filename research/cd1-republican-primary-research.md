## 1. RON RUSSELL — Platform Positions

### Economy/Jobs/Taxes
- Top priority is the economy. "I think our disaffected population is our young people and our older people on a fixed income, and some of the benefits in the great big beautiful bill are not being taken advantage of by those target populations." — [Maine Morning Star, 5/26/26](https://mainemorningstar.com/2026/05/26/in-1st-district-primary-republicans-diverge-on-their-approach-to-trump/)
- "We need to control the supply of oil on the market, and we can do that very easily with what we have underneath our feet." — [WMTW, 2024 candidate profile](https://www.wmtw.com/article/maine-1st-congressional-district-republican-candidate-ron-russell/62558954)
- "Maine is not a business-friendly state right now when our taxes are very, very high." — [WMTW, 2024](https://www.wmtw.com/article/maine-1st-congressional-district-republican-candidate-ron-russell/62558954)
- Supports lowering taxes to increase business in Maine. — [WMTW, 2024](https://www.wmtw.com/article/maine-1st-congressional-district-republican-candidate-ron-russell/62558954)

### Healthcare/Medicaid
- "I plan on ensuring that Medicaid is available to those who need it by eliminating the fraud, waste, and abuse that is occurring in the system. The expansion of Mainecare to include illegal immigrants has increased the costs to the program and needs to be eliminated." — [Maine Public, 4/17/26](https://www.mainepublic.org/politics/2026-04-17/your-vote-2026-profile-ron-russell-republican-for-1st-district)
- Would have voted FOR the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, even with Medicaid cuts: "I would have voted for the bill, because during Covid the applicability of Medicare, Medicaid, increased more than it should have been prior to Covid." — [Maine Morning Star, 5/22/26](https://mainemorningstar.com/2026/05/22/at-first-and-only-debate-1st-district-republicans-square-off-on-healthcare-immigration/)
- Would NOT support reinstating Affordable Care Act tax credits that expired, causing marketplace plan prices to rise: "Healthcare needs to be looked at, because that's one of the largest expenses we have in our federal budget and our state budget." — [Maine Morning Star, 5/22/26](https://mainemorningstar.com/2026/05/22/at-first-and-only-debate-1st-district-republicans-square-off-on-healthcare-immigration/)

### Immigration
- Supports building a wall on the southern border: "I would like to see Congress finish building a wall along the southern border to stop all illegal immigration coming into the United States." — [Maine Morning Star, 2024 debate](https://mainemorningstar.com/2024/10/17/maines-cd1-candidates-talk-climate-change-cost-of-living-in-televised-debate/)
- On the January 2026 ICE operation in Maine: "I don't necessarily find the administration at fault." He pointed to lack of cooperation between Maine law enforcement and ICE as the misstep. "If state and local and federal authorities had talked, they would have agreed that he either needed to be picked up or not needed to be picked up." — [Maine Morning Star, 5/22/26](https://mainemorningstar.com/2026/05/22/at-first-and-only-debate-1st-district-republicans-square-off-on-healthcare-immigration/)
- On affordable housing (immigration angle): "I notice here in southern Maine, affordable housing has a negative connotation to it. Folks think that we're housing illegal immigrants only in affordable housing." — [WMTW, 2024](https://www.wmtw.com/article/ronald-russell-republican-candidate-maine-1st-congressional-district/60992030)

### Iran/War
- Supports the U.S. military campaign against Iran: "I think it has been beneficial, and it will continue to be beneficial for the citizens of the United States, because we have been held hostage by Iran for 49 years." — [Maine Morning Star, 5/22/26](https://mainemorningstar.com/2026/05/22/at-first-and-only-debate-1st-district-republicans-square-off-on-healthcare-immigration/)
- Would not take boots-on-the-ground off the table: "I don't think that I would take boots on the ground off the table as an option, I think all of the options ought to be available to the president." — [Maine Morning Star, 5/22/26](https://mainemorningstar.com/2026/05/22/at-first-and-only-debate-1st-district-republicans-square-off-on-healthcare-immigration/)
- Spoke with NEWS CENTER Maine about "the rising cost of living sparked by the war with Iran, ways to lower energy costs." — [NEWS CENTER Maine](https://www.newscentermaine.com/article/news/local/207/207-interview/the-207-interview-cd1-republican-primary-candidates/97-0696651d-6576-453e-87d1-458a7cb829fd)

## 4. DEBATE HIGHLIGHTS & DISAGREEMENTS

Two debates were held:
1. **May 21, 2026** — Maine Public / Portland Press Herald debate in Lewiston. Moderated by Jen Rooks; questions from Steve Mistler (Maine Public) and Billy Kobin (Press Herald). — [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/video/cd1-republican-primary-debate-hgdlpk/)
2. **NEWS CENTER Maine "Voice of the Voter" debate** — moderated by Phil Hirschkorn. — [NEWS CENTER Maine](https://www.newscentermaine.com/article/news/politics/maine-politics/watch-voice-of-the-voter-first-congressional-district-gop-primary-debate-maine/97-58a803c1-ef23-4f44-86fa-1c34adecf3d7)

### Key Disagreements

#### Iran / Foreign Policy
- **Russell:** Supports the military campaign; would keep boots-on-the-ground as an option; "we have been held hostage by Iran for 49 years."
- **Pietrowicz:** Opposes ground invasion; "I don't think the American people have the stomach for Iraq and Afghanistan 2.0"; supports naval blockade only; would only support ground troops with credible, public intelligence.
- This was the sharpest contrast in the debate.

#### Healthcare / Medicaid
- **Russell:** Supports One Big Beautiful Bill with its Medicaid cuts; opposes ACA tax credit reinstatement; wants to cut fraud/waste/abuse and eliminate MaineCare expansion to undocumented immigrants.
- **Pietrowicz:** Opposes One Big Beautiful Bill as written; supports ACA tax credits as a bridge; supports a universal healthcare hybrid; wants to protect vulnerable populations from Big Pharma.

#### Immigration Enforcement
- **Russell:** Doesn't fault the administration for January ICE operation; blames lack of coordination between state/local/federal authorities.
- **Pietrowicz:** Calls for stronger oversight of immigration officers and accountability including prison time for failing officials.

#### Trump
- **Russell:** Closer alignment; says providing support to Trump is "the biggest thing"; criticizes Trump's style (how he treats departing personnel) but not his policies.
- **Pietrowicz:** Willing to be critical; "I don't like his style"; called out Trump's acceptance of the Qatar jet; still says he'd vote for Trump over Harris.

#### Electability / Experience
- **Pietrowicz's critique:** "In 2024, Colonel Russell lost his district by 95,000+ votes. And that is a margin that no Republican in the last decade has been able to crack 37%. We need a fresh face. We need new energy, new blood." — [PBS debate](https://www.pbs.org/video/cd1-republican-primary-debate-hgdlpk/)
- **Russell's counter:** "Don't confuse enthusiasm with capability. I'm the one with the experience and the capability to be successful in Washington, D.C." — [Maine Morning Star, 5/26/26](https://mainemorningstar.com/2026/05/26/in-1st-district-primary-republicans-diverge-on-their-approach-to-trump/)

#### Ukraine
- **Russell:** Supports aid but not a "blank check."
- **Pietrowicz:** Opposes more active role; "I certainly don't support sending billions overseas"; thinks Zelenskyy must make compromises.
