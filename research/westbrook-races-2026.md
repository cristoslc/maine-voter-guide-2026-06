## Structured, nonpartisan research for voter guide. Sources cited throughout.

## 0. Scope & Methodology

This document covers races on the **June 9, 2026 Maine State Primary ballot that affect voters in the City of Westbrook, Maine** (Cumberland County):

- **State Senate Districts 26 and 27** (the two SDs that geographically cover Westbrook; Duson's SD 27 and Nangle's SD 26 each include part of Westbrook)
- **State House Districts 126, 127, 128** (all three Westbrook districts — confirmed via the official Maine Legislature Towns-in-District list)
- **Cumberland County races** that appear on Westbrook voters' ballots (District Attorney District 2, County Sheriff, Judge of Probate, County Commissioner District 4) — covered in the project's existing `portland-races-2026.md` and South Portland research
- **Statewide races** (US Senate, Governor, CD1) are covered in the project's existing research files; not duplicated here.
- **Westbrook Municipal School Budget Validation Referendum** — *the only local ballot question on the Westbrook June 9 ballot.*

> **Important corrections to the original task prompt:** The user's prompt assumed Westbrook was in State Senate District 28 and House Districts 125/126/127. That is incorrect. Per the official Maine Legislature district pages and the SOS candidate list:
> - **SD 28 = part of Portland + Long Island** (Sen. Rachel Talbot Ross). It does **not** include Westbrook.
> - **Westbrook is in SD 26 (Nangle, D) and SD 27 (Duson, D)**.
> - **Westbrook's House districts are 126, 127, 128**, not 125/126/127. HD 125 (Kelly Noonan Murphy, D) is a **Scarborough** district.

## 2. State Senate District 26 — Contested Republican Primary

**Geography**: Casco, Frye Island, Raymond, Windham, and **part of Westbrook**. ~27,197 actively registered voters across the full district. [Maine Monitor, SD 26](https://themainemonitor.org/maine-state-senate-district-26/)

**Voter composition (2024 cycle)**: District-wide roughly even (R, D, and unenrolled each ~⅓). In the Westbrook portion of the district specifically, **Democrats have a strong enrollment advantage** (45% D vs. 29% unenrolled vs. 20% R) — making Westbrook the largest single reservoir of Democratic votes for Sen. Nangle. [Press Herald 10/26/24](https://www.pressherald.com/2024/10/26/windham-finds-itself-on-the-front-line-in-battle-for-maine-legislature/)

**District competitiveness**: This is one of the most expensive Maine Senate races in recent cycles. In 2024, Nangle defeated Cianchette 52%–48% (12,124 vs. 11,177 votes), with combined party/outside spending of ~$170,000. Democrats are defending a 9-seat Senate majority; Republicans need to flip only 3 seats to take over. [Press Herald 10/22/24](https://www.pressherald.com/2024/10/22/these-are-the-most-expensive-races-for-the-maine-house-and-senate/), [Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/Kenneth_Cianchette)

### 2a. Democratic Primary — Timothy Nangle (D), Unopposed

**Candidate**: **Timothy E. Nangle (D)** — incumbent. [Maine Legislature](https://legislature.maine.gov/district26) · [Maine Monitor, SD 26](https://themainemonitor.org/maine-state-senate-district-26/) · [Windham Eagle Q&A 2024](https://news.thewindhameagle.com/2024/10/maine-senate-district-26-candidate.html)

**Background**
- 58 years old. **Help desk technician** (IT). [Windham Eagle]
- Born in Boxford, Massachusetts; became a paramedic in 1988; moved to Maine in 1990.
- **27-year career as a Medcu Paramedic at Portland Fire Department** before retiring.
- **Two terms on the Windham Town Council** before being elected to the Maine Senate in 2022 (131st Legislature); re-elected 2024.
- Senate committees: **Transportation (Chair), Health and Human Services**. [Maine Legislature]

**Key Legislative Work / Platform Issues** (per Windham Eagle Q&A, 2024 — most current direct voter-facing positions available)
- **Housing**: "Housing, Housing and Housing. The high cost of housing has impacted renters and buyers all across the state." Supported expanding affordable rental and ownership options; supported mobile-home-park resident protections; supported the **Stable Homes** emergency rental-assistance program; supported LD 1422 (homeless students) and a budget with $100M for low-income/workforce housing. [Windham Eagle]
- **Property-tax relief**: Supported the **Property Tax Fairness Credit** expansion, which raises eligibility and the maximum benefit for older Mainers. [Windham Eagle]
- **State-meeting voter mandate**: Pointed to delivering the **voter-mandated 55% state funding of public education** "for the first time" as a Democratic accomplishment. [Press Herald 10/26/24]
- **Shoreland zoning enforcement**: Sponsored **LD 2101** (signed into law) — lets municipalities restrict/revoke permits and place liens on properties with unresolved shoreland zoning violations; requires disclosure to buyers. Already helped Raymond. [Windham Eagle]
- **Fiscal posture**: "We have done a good job maintaining fiscal responsibility and continuing to invest in worthwhile programs and infrastructure"; filled the **Rainy Day Fund to >$900M**. Defended the budget process against Republican "runaway spending" charges, calling a projected $637M general-fund gap "routinely addressed through the budget process." [Press Herald 10/26/24]
- **Veterans and long-term care**: Championed $5.1M for Maine Veterans' Homes and $26M to keep long-term facilities open. [Windham Eagle]
- **Healthcare/aging**: Expanded the **Medicare Savings Programs**; protected older Mainers from utility shutoffs in extreme weather. [Windham Eagle]

**Controversies / contested votes**
- 2024 Republican ad campaign targeted him for his vote ending the **Property Tax Stabilization for Seniors** program (which Republicans had created and which proved unsustainable for municipalities and the state; the program extended benefits to wealthy seniors and was replaced by expanded existing programs in a bipartisan 11-1 committee vote). [Press Herald 10/26/24]
- Faced Republican pressure on the **school funding formula** vote in 2025-26 cycle; minority leader Faulkingham cited the $44M price tag of recent formula changes as opposition rationale. [Maine Morning Star 6/1/26]

**Endorsements (2026 cycle)**
- Maine Senate Democrats (incumbent). [Maine Legislature]
- Maine AFL-CIO, Maine Conservation Voters, Maine People's Alliance, Planned Parenthood Maine Action Fund (per 2024 pattern; project to confirm in primary cycle)

### 2b. Republican Primary — **CONTESTED**: Kenneth Cianchette vs. Peter Violette

#### 2b-i. Kenneth "Kenny" J. Cianchette (R) — Challenger

**Candidate**: **Kenneth J. Cianchette (R)**. Residence: Windham, ME. Age 37. [Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/Kenneth_Cianchette) · [Press Herald 10/26/24](https://www.pressherald.com/2024/10/26/windham-finds-itself-on-the-front-line-in-battle-for-maine-legislature/) · [Windham Eagle 2024 Q&A](https://news.thewindhameagle.com/2024/10/maine-senate-district-26-candidate.html)

**Background**
- Lifelong Mainer, born in Portland. Bachelor's, University of Maine, 2010. Catholic.
- **Owner of Erik's Church**, a Windham restaurant and concert venue, for ~7 years (through the 2024 cycle). Also serves as general manager, property manager, project manager. Affiliated with **Sebago Lake Region Chamber of Commerce** and **Hospitality Maine**. [Ballotpedia]
- **2024 challenger**: Lost to Nangle 48%–52% (11,177 to 12,124). 2024 campaign raised **$69,684**, spent $67,194. [Ballotpedia]
- Was a registered **unenrolled voter** until recruited to run as Republican in 2024. [Press Herald 10/26/24]
- Models himself on **Bill Diamond**, the late Democratic state senator who previously held SD 26. Diamond's family is now backing Cianchette. [Maine Morning Star 6/1/26]

**Key Platform Issues** (2024 cycle, restated for 2026 per debate and Ballotpedia Candidate Connection)
- **Cost of living / economy**: "Runaway costs of living" = top issue. Plans to grow the tax base through "responsible development" to relieve property-tax pressure on homeowners. [Press Herald 10/26/24]
- **Taxes**: Wants to **eliminate income tax on the first $75,000 of gross earned income** (proposed first bill if elected). Wants stronger **homestead exemption** and explicit property-tax protections for retired seniors. Wants to **shift tax burden from residents to visitors** to generate new state revenue. [Ballotpedia 2024 Candidate Connection; Windham Eagle]
- **Child welfare / foster care** (Diamond's signature issue; the Diamond family endorsement rationale): supports major child welfare reform — the late Sen. Bill Diamond long pushed these reforms. [Maine Morning Star 6/1/26]
- **Education**: Skeptical of "spending more money will make things better" orthodoxy; wants results-tied funding and supports a mix of public, private, and higher-ed models. [Ballotpedia 2024]
- **Style / governing philosophy** (the Cianchette vs. Violette fault line): Self-described **"fighter"** — "We need a fighter… I know, Peter's a nice guy, and I know he's gonna go up there and kumbaya everybody. Bill Diamond was the king of being a statesman. He's far better than either of us, and he couldn't get it done because it's a new type of politics." [Maine Morning Star 6/1/26]
- **Abortion rights**: Says he supports a woman's right to choose, but in a Christian Civic League questionnaire opposed adding abortion rights to the Maine Constitution, saying he couldn't commit without seeing the actual language. [Press Herald 10/26/24]
- **Emergency powers**: Wants to limit governor's emergency powers to 7 days at a time. [Ballotpedia 2024]

**Controversies**
- 2024 Republican ads on the **senior property-tax freeze** program (he supported ending the program and replacing it with broader tax credits). [Press Herald 10/26/24]
- Internal-GOP contrast: Critics within the party (and Violette in the primary) characterize his approach as a **"football coach mentality"** that won't produce results. [Maine Morning Star 6/1/26]
- Has not completed the 2026 Ballotpedia Candidate Connection survey as of 5/9/26. [Ballotpedia]

**Endorsements**
- **Diamond family** (late Sen. Bill Diamond's family) — major Democratic-tradition endorsement of a Republican. [Maine Morning Star 6/1/26]
- Sebago Lake Region Chamber of Commerce (per his business affiliations, 2024)
- Did not get a public nod from U.S. Term Limits (Violette did). [U.S. Term Limits 1/27/26]

**General Election (Nov 2026)**
- If Cianchette wins the GOP primary, he faces Nangle. 2024 result was within 4 points; this is a top-tier Maine Senate flip target per the University of Virginia Center for Politics. [Press Herald 10/26/24]

[Maine Senior Guide](https://maineseniorguide.com/member/comfort-keepers/) · [Muddy Waters Podcast, 3/16/26](https://podcasts.apple.com/au/podcast/ep-154-peter-violette-from-logger-to-senior-care-owner/id1773387345?i=1000755566531)

- **First Black woman to serve in the Maine Senate** (elected 2022). [Maine Senate Democrats]

- **Senate Assistant Majority Leader**. Committees: Government Oversight, Veterans and Legal Affairs, Senate Rules. [Maine Monitor]
- Daughter of civil rights pioneer Gerald E. Talbot (Maine's first Black legislator); sister-in-law of Sen. Rachel Talbot Ross (SD 28). Gerald E. Talbot died May 9, 2026. [Maine Senate Democrats, 5/12/26]

**Key Legislative Work / Platform Issues**
- Sponsored **tenant privacy/retaliation protections** (signed into law April 22, 2026). [Maine Senate Democrats 4/23/26]

- **Founding Chair, Blue Economy Task Force** (marine economy / coastal workforce). [Maine Senate Democrats]
- Focus on "safe, affordable and accessible housing options in Portland." [Maine Senate Democrats]
- Supports **Wabanaki tribal sovereignty**; the Blue Economy Task Force included tribal representation.
- Defended CMP corridor in prior lobbying work — campaign issue in past progressive challenges. [Wikipedia]

**Controversies**

- **CMP lobbying**: Worked as a Central Maine Power lobbyist between council stints; CMP-corridor opponents have used this against her. [Wikipedia]
- Lost 2018 Duson vs. Sanborn D primary (≈41%–59%) over CMP corridor and other issues; ran again and won in 2022. [Wikipedia]

**Endorsements (2026 cycle)**
- Maine Senate Democrats. [Maine Senate Democrats]
- Maine AFL-CIO, Maine Conservation Voters Action Fund (per 2024 pattern; project to confirm in primary cycle)

**General Election (Nov 2026)**

## 7. Westbrook School Budget Validation Referendum

> **The only local ballot question on the Westbrook June 9 ballot.** [Westbrook Elections](https://www.westbrookmaine.gov/181/Elections-Voting) · [Press Herald 5/5/26](https://www.pressherald.com/2026/05/05/westbrook-city-council-approves-106-5-million-budget-package/)

**Question (verbatim, per city)**:
> "Do you favor approving the Westbrook School Department budget for the upcoming school year that was adopted at the latest Westbrook City Council school budget meeting?" [Westbrook Elections]

**Headline figures**:
- **$57.2 million** Westbrook School Department budget for FY27.
- **7.5% increase** over the previous year (FY26's $54M).
- **Initial proposal was $58.6 million (≈10% increase)**; the council's Finance Committee reduced it to $57.2M.
- Westbrook City Council approved the full $106.5M combined municipal/school budget **6–0 on May 4, 2026** (one member absent, Gary Rairdon, who had said "I will not be approving either budgets" at the April 27 first reading).
- Combined budget: **$106.5M**; municipal portion $49.3M, school portion $57.2M. Of the combined budget, **$61.7M raised in taxes**.
- **Estimated tax-rate impact**: +$1.15 per $1,000 of assessed property value → total **$16.25/$1,000**, ≈7.61% higher than the current rate. [Press Herald 5/5/26]

**Drivers of the school-budget increase**:
- Preschool expansion.
- Rising health-care costs.
- **Reduction of Title I federal funds**. [Press Herald 5/5/26]

**Public comment at council**:
- A few residents said "taxpayers aren't getting their money's worth."
- Councilors noted increasing student populations and a high population of students with special education needs.
- **Mayor David Morse** said Westbrook has the **4th lowest per-pupil spending** out of all Cumberland County school districts. [Press Herald 5/5/26]

**Recent history of Westbrook school-budget referendums** (per Press Herald 5/5/26):
- **2025 (FY26)**: $54M passed.
- **2024 (FY25)**: First $51.7M **rejected**; then $51M passed.
- **2023**: required four votes; passed in November by 50 votes.
- (WMTW 6/2024) [WMTW 6/2024](https://www.wmtw.com/article/voters-multiple-maine-districts-reject-school-budgets/61083252)

**Why the validation referendum exists**:
> "The Westbrook School Department's portion of the City requires an additional layer of approval before final adoption — the approval of voters at a School Budget Validation Referendum." [City of Westbrook FY 2025-2026 Budget](https://www.westbrookmaine.gov/823/FY-2025-2026-Budget)

**No other June 9 ballot questions for Westbrook**:
- No municipal charter questions.
- No city council or school board races (those are November).
- No state bond questions or citizen initiatives on the Maine June 9 primary ballot.
- The Westbrook City Charter is governed by the City Council; there is no current charter-revision referendum pending.
