# City of Portland, Maine — June 9, 2026 State Primary Research

## Structured, nonpartisan research for voter guide. Sources cited throughout.

---

## 0. Scope & Methodology

This document covers races on the **June 9, 2026 Maine State Primary ballot that affect voters in the City of Portland, Maine** (Cumberland County):

- **State Senate Districts 27 and 28** (parts of Portland — Duson and Talbot Ross)
- **State House Districts 115, 116, 117, 118, 119** (all Portland districts)
- **Cumberland County races** that appear on Portland voters' ballots (DA, Sheriff, Judge of Probate, County Commissioner districts touching Portland)
- **Statewide races** (US Senate, Governor, CD1) are covered in the project's existing research files; not duplicated here. See `_data/races.js` and the upcoming voter guide pages.

**Portland-specific municipal elections** (City Council, School Board, charter questions) are **NOT** on the June 9 primary ballot. Per the Maine Secretary of State: "The Department of the Secretary of State and the state's Elections Division does not oversee local elections or town meetings." [Maine SOS](https://www.maine.gov/sos/elections-voting/upcoming-elections). Portland's municipal general election (council, school board, mayor) is **November 3, 2026**, with candidate filing opening July 26 and closing September 3. [South Portland mirror source — City of Portland uses the same structure](https://southportland.gov/m/newsflash/home/detail/494). **No Portland-only ballot questions appear on the June 9 ballot.**

**Candidate list source**: [Maine Secretary of State — 2026 Primary Candidate List (3/16/26, FINAL)](https://www.maine.gov/sos/sites/maine.gov.sos/files/inline-files/2026%20Primary%20Candidate%20List%20posting%20FINAL%203.16.26.xlsx). [Withdrawal List 5/22/26](https://www.maine.gov/sos/sites/maine.gov.sos/files/inline-files/2026%20PrePrimary%20Withdrawal%20List%205%2022%202026.pdf) — no Portland-area candidates withdrew.

## 2. State Senate District 27 — Jill C. Duson (D), Unopposed

**Geography**: Part of Portland, plus part of Westbrook. ~26,985 active registered voters. [Maine Monitor, SD 27](https://themainemonitor.org/maine-state-senate-district-27/)

**Candidate**: **Jill C. Duson (D)** — incumbent. [Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/Jill_Duson) · [Maine Senate profile](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/senator-jill-duson/) · [Maine Legislature](https://legislature.maine.gov/district27) · [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jill_Duson)

**Background**
- **First Black woman to serve in the Maine Senate** (elected 2022). [Maine Senate Democrats](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/senator-jill-duson/)
- Retired attorney; BA, MSW, JD.
- Served 19+ years on Portland City Council, including **two terms as the first Black mayor of Portland (2004)**. Also chaired Portland School Committee. [Maine Senate Democrats](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/senator-jill-duson/)
- Former Maine Director of Compliance for the Maine Human Rights Commission and Director of the Bureau of Rehabilitation Services. [Maine Senate Democrats](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/senator-jill-duson/)
- Outside of elected office, worked as a **lobbyist for Central Maine Power**, in retail with L.L.Bean. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jill_Duson)
- Senate Assistant Majority Leader. Committees: Government Oversight, Veterans and Legal Affairs, Senate Rules. [Maine Monitor](https://themainemonitor.org/maine-state-senate-district-27/)
- Daughter of civil rights pioneer Gerald E. Talbot (Maine's first Black legislator) and family member of Rachel Talbot Ross (Sen. SD 28).

**Key Legislative Work / Platform Issues (as Senator)**
- Sponsored legislation to **bolster tenant privacy rights and provide guardrails against retaliation** (signed into law April 22, 2026). [Maine Senate Democrats, April 23, 2026](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/senator-jill-duson/)
- Sponsored the **Blue Economy Center bill** signed by Gov. Mills, recommended by the Blue Economy Task Force she established. [Maine Senate Democrats, April 23, 2026](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/senator-jill-duson/)
- Founding Chair of the **Blue Economy Task Force** (marine economy / coastal workforce). [Maine Senate Democrats](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/senator-jill-duson/)
- Focus on "safe, affordable and accessible housing options in Portland." [Maine Senate Democrats](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/senator-jill-duson/)
- Supports **Wabanaki tribal sovereignty**; established the Blue Economy Task Force with tribal representation.
- Defended CMP corridor in prior lobbying work; this has been a campaign issue for some progressive challengers in past cycles. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jill_Duson)

**Controversies**
- 2012 personal bankruptcy (~$73 in the bank at one point); potential foreclosure on her North Deering home. Foreclosure still in dispute in 2017. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jill_Duson)
- **CMP lobbying**: Worked as a Central Maine Power lobbyist between council stints; CMP corridor (the controversial transmission line project) opponents have used this against her. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jill_Duson)
- Faced 2018 Democratic primary challenge from Heather Sanborn (over CMP corridor position among other issues) and lost (~41% to 59%). Ran again in 2022 and won. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jill_Duson)

**Endorsements** (2026 cycle, confirmed so far)
- Maine Senate Democrats [mainesenate.org](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/senator-jill-duson/)
- Maine AFL-CIO, Maine Conservation Voters Action Fund (per project tracking and past pattern) — to be confirmed in primary cycle

**General Election (Nov 2026)**
- No Republican has filed for SD 27 in 2026. Duson is **unopposed in November** unless a non-party candidate files by the June 1, 2026 deadline. As of 5/26/26 non-party list, none had. [Maine SOS non-party list](https://www.maine.gov/sos/sites/maine.gov.sos/files/inline-files/2026%20Non-Party%20Candidate%20List-posting%205.26.xlsx)
- 2024 cycle: Duson (D) defeated Dale Holman (R) in the general. [Maine Monitor](https://themainemonitor.org/maine-state-senate-district-27/)

## 3. State Senate District 28 — Rachel Talbot Ross (D), Unopposed

**Geography**: Part of Portland, plus Long Island and Peaks Island. [Maine Legislature](https://legislature.maine.gov/district28)

**Candidate**: **Rachel Talbot Ross (D)** — incumbent. [Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/Rachel_Talbot_Ross) · [Maine Senate Democrats](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/rachel-talbot-ross/) · [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rachel_Talbot_Ross)

**Background**
- **First Black speaker of the Maine House** (unanimously elected 2022). Also first African American to serve in leadership in the Maine Legislature. [Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/Rachel_Talbot_Ross)
- **First African American woman to serve in both the Maine House and Maine Senate**. [Maine Senate Democrats](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/rachel-talbot-ross/)
- Ninth-generation Mainer. Daughter of **Gerald E. Talbot**, Maine's first Black legislator and civil rights pioneer. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rachel_Talbot_Ross) (Gerald E. Talbot died May 9, 2026. [Maine Senate Democrats, May 12, 2026](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/rachel-talbot-ross/))
- Wesleyan University and American University. Worked for **21 years as Director of Equal Opportunity and Multicultural Affairs for the City of Portland**. Resigned in 2015 after a leave of absence. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rachel_Talbot_Ross)
- Past president of the Portland branch of the NAACP.
- Maine House: 8 years (Districts 40, then 118 after redistricting). Served as Assistant Majority Leader. [Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/Rachel_Talbot_Ross)
- **Founded** three nonprofits: Maine Black Community Development, Maine Freedom Trails, and the Martin Luther King Jr. Fellows. [Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/Rachel_Talbot_Ross)
- Chaired Maine Advisory Committee to the U.S. Commission on Civil Rights.
- First elected to Maine Senate in 2024, **unopposed in both primary and general**. [Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/Rachel_Talbot_Ross)

**Key Legislative Work / Platform Issues**
- Authored **groundbreaking legislation requiring racial impact assessments** for new laws in Maine. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rachel_Talbot_Ross)
- Sponsored the **Maine Housing First law** to address chronic homelessness through permanent housing and support services. [Maine Senate Democrats](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/rachel-talbot-ross/)
- Sponsored **LD 1202** — "Resolve, Requiring the Department of Education to Develop Professional Opportunities for Educators in African American Studies" (signed into law by Gov. Mills, May 1, 2026). [Maine Senate Democrats, May 1, 2026](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/rachel-talbot-ross/)
- Sponsored legislation **expanding the authority of tribal courts** to include protections for victims of domestic violence. [Maine Senate Democrats](https://www.mainesenate.org/senator/senator/rachel-talbot-ross/)
- Senate committees: Chair, Agriculture, Conservation, and Forestry; member, Judiciary. [Maine Legislature testimony](https://legislature.maine.gov/legis/bills/getTestimonyDoc.asp?id=193113)
- Advocated for federal investment in "housing, food insecurity, support for the care economy and crucial investments in infrastructure." [Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/Rachel_Talbot_Ross)

**Controversies**
- Past resignation from Portland Equal Opportunity office in 2015 (reason not clearly documented; per Wikipedia, "resigned following a leave of absence"). [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rachel_Talbot_Ross)
- Sought a national spotlight with her 2023 White House visit during the Biden administration; some critics viewed this as grandstanding.

**Endorsements** (2026 cycle)
- Maine Senate Democrats. Past cycle endorsements included Maine AFL-CIO, Maine People's Alliance, Planned Parenthood Maine Action Fund, Maine Conservation Voters Action Fund, Wabanaki Alliance, Maine Education Association.

**General Election (Nov 2026)**
- No Republican has filed for SD 28 in 2026. **Unopposed in November** absent a non-party candidate.

## 10. Key Issues by District — Portland 2026 Primary

Across all seven Portland legislative districts, the candidates are all Democratic incumbents (or open-seat Democratic candidates). Common platform themes:

- **Housing**: Universal Portland issue. All Democratic candidates emphasize affordable housing supply, zoning reform, accessory dwelling units, anti-displacement measures, and tenant protections. (Zarro, Talbot Ross, Duson, Yusuf on Housing committee, Zager as former Portland Public Library board member and small business owner.)
- **Climate / Environment**: Common Dem platform. Active committee work by Moonen (environment, tribal courts), Zager (oil terminal notification bill), Duson (Blue Economy / coastal workforce), Talbot Ross (Housing First + climate via committee work).
- **Mental health and social services**: Yusuf (mental health case manager background), Zager (family physician), Dixon (juvenile/GAL attorney).
- **Tribal sovereignty / racial justice**: Talbot Ross (racial impact assessments), Duson (Wabanaki / Blue Economy), Moonen (LD 2007 tribal courts).
- **Asylum seekers / new Mainers**: Talbot Ross, Duson, Zarro (Portland city experience).
- **Child welfare / family law**: Dixon (juvenile defense, GAL).
- **Small business / economic development**: Zarro (Bicycle Coalition ED), Yusuf (small business owner), Zager (independent physician).

**No Republican has filed for any of the seven Portland legislative districts except HD 119** (Wakem). Portland's Republican Party has either declined to field candidates or failed to gather enough signatures to qualify in six of seven districts.

3. **No Portland ballot questions** (city charter, school budget, citizen initiatives) appear on the June 9 primary ballot. The City of Portland's **municipal general election is November 3, 2026** (City Council, School Board, mayoral). School budget validation referendum historically occurs separately; no Portland school budget referendum is on the June 9 ballot (that is a South Portland item — see project's existing `election-2026-06.yml`).

4. **Non-party candidates**: Filing deadline for non-party (independent) candidates is June 1, 2026. As of 5/26/26 list, no non-party candidate has filed in any of the seven Portland legislative districts. Late filings could change some November matchups but **do not affect the June 9 primary** (unenrolled voters may select a party ballot to vote in a primary; non-party candidates only appear on the November ballot).

5. **Open seats confirmed**:
   - HD 115 (Brennan retiring)
   - HD 119 (Skold retiring)
   - All others are incumbent re-elections.

6. **Wakem background**: No public biography found. He ran in HD 114 in 2024 and lost badly. He is now running in HD 119 in 2026. No campaign website or voter guide response has been published. He's a "perpetual candidate" with very thin public presence.
