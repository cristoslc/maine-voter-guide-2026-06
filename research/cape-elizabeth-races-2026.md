# Town of Cape Elizabeth, Maine — June 9, 2026 State Primary Research

## Structured, nonpartisan research for voter guide. Sources cited throughout.

---

## 0. Scope & Methodology

This document covers races and ballot questions on the **June 9, 2026 Maine State Primary ballot that affect voters in the Town of Cape Elizabeth, Maine** (Cumberland County):

- **State Senate District 29** (Sen. Anne Carney, D — Cape Elizabeth / South Portland / part of Scarborough) — entire town
- **State House Districts 121 and 123** — Cape Elizabeth is split between these two districts (not HD 120, 122 — see correction below)
- **Cumberland County Commissioner District 4** — contested Democratic primary (Cape Elizabeth resident Olson vs. South Portland resident Smith)
- **Cape Elizabeth School Department FY27 Budget Validation Referendum** — only Cape Elizabeth-specific ballot question confirmed for June 9
- **Statewide races** (US Senate, Governor, CD1) are covered in the project's existing research files; not duplicated here.

> **⚠️ Major correction to the original task prompt:** The user's prompt stated Cape Elizabeth is in "State House Districts 120, 121, 122 (Dem + Rep for each)." **This is incorrect.** Per the official Maine Legislature "Towns in Each District" list (132nd Legislature, current as of May 29, 2026 — see [legislature.maine.gov/house/MemberProfiles/ListDistrictTowns](https://legislature.maine.gov/house/MemberProfiles/ListDistrictTowns)) and the official [SOS Candidate List (3/16/26 FINAL)](https://www.maine.gov/sos/sites/maine.gov.sos/files/inline-files/2026%20Primary%20Candidate%20List%20posting%20FINAL%203.16.26.xlsx):

> |---|---|---|
> | **Cape Elizabeth (part)** | **29** | **121** (also South Portland part) |
> | **Cape Elizabeth (part)** | **29** | **123** (Cape Elizabeth only) |
> | South Portland (part) | 29 | 120 (South Portland only) |
> | South Portland (part) | 29 | 121 (also Cape Elizabeth part) |
> | South Portland (part) | 29 | 122 (South Portland only) |
>
> **HD 120 (Dougherty/Shedlock) and HD 122 (Beck) do NOT include Cape Elizabeth.** They are South Portland-only districts. Conversely, **HD 123 (Boyer D, Christy R) is the Cape Elizabeth-only district that the user's prompt did not list at all.** The current incumbents for these districts in the 132nd Legislature are: Deqa Dhalac (HD 120, D), Christopher Kessler (HD 121, D, term-limited), Matthew Beck (HD 122, D), and Michelle Boyer (HD 123, D).

> **⚠️ HD 121 is an OPEN SEAT in 2026.** Rep. Christopher Kessler (4 terms, 129th–132nd Legislature) is term-limited in 2026 — Robert Cameron (D) is the new Democratic candidate for the seat Kessler is vacating. Cameron is from South Portland. He currently has no public campaign website, Ballotpedia profile photo, or VOTE411/Ballotpedia Candidate Connection response as of 6/1/26.

**Cape Elizabeth-specific ballot questions on June 9, 2026:**
- **YES: Cape Elizabeth School Department FY27 Budget Validation Referendum** — confirmed by [Cape Elizabeth FY27 Budget Schedule](https://www.capeelizabeth.gov/departments/finance/annual_budgets.php), which lists "Citizen Vote on Town Council Adopted School Budget" for **Tuesday, June 9, 2026**. Cape Elizabeth has its **own** school department (Cape Elizabeth School Department, separate from South Portland School Department) — this is **not** the same referendum as the South Portland one.
- **Cape Elizabeth Town Council and School Board elections: NOT on the June 9 primary ballot.** Per the Town of Cape Elizabeth website and Maine election law, municipal elections occur in November. The seven-member Town Council is currently seated (Andrew Swayze, Stephanie Anderson, Penny Jordan, Elizabeth Scifres, Caitlin Harriman, Jonathan Sahrbeck, Tim Thompson) and is not up for election in June. Two school board seats (Voltz 2026, McVeigh 2026) will be on the November ballot.

## 6. Cape Elizabeth-Specific Ballot Questions — June 9, 2026

### 6a. Cape Elizabeth School Department FY27 Budget Validation Referendum — **YES, ON JUNE 9 BALLOT**

**This is the ONLY Cape Elizabeth-specific ballot question on the June 9, 2026 primary.**

Per the official Town of Cape Elizabeth FY27 Budget Review Schedule (approved 12/8/2025, published at [capeelizabeth.gov/departments/finance/annual_budgets.php](https://www.capeelizabeth.gov/departments/finance/annual_budgets.php)):

> **"Citizen Vote on Town Council Adopted School Budget — Tuesday, June 9, 2026"**

**Context and process**:
- Cape Elizabeth has its **own** School Department (Cape Elizabeth School Department, [cape.k12.me.us](https://www.cape.k12.me.us/)) — separate from South Portland School Department. **Cape Elizabeth is NOT in the South Portland School Department.** This means the South Portland FY27 budget referendum on June 9 is a different question and only applies to South Portland voters.
- The Cape Elizabeth School Department is governed by a 7-member School Board (3-year terms); budget is then approved by the Cape Elizabeth Town Council and submitted to a **citizen referendum for validation** (per Maine law — school budget validation).
- **FY27 schedule (Cape Elizabeth)**:
  - School Board recommended budget delivered to Town Council: **Monday, April 17, 2026**
  - Budget Public Comments and School Board Budget Presentation at Town Council meeting: **Monday, April 27, 2026**
  - Public Hearing FY 2027 at Town Council meeting: **Monday, May 4, 2026**
  - Vote on the FY 2027 Budget at Special Town Council Meeting: **Wednesday, May 6, 2026**
  - **Citizen Vote on Town Council Adopted School Budget: Tuesday, June 9, 2026** [Cape Elizabeth Finance]
- **District**: 1,525 students (2023-24), 3 schools (Cape Elizabeth High School, Cape Elizabeth Middle School, Pond Cove Elementary). [Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/Cape_Elizabeth_School_Department,_Maine)
- **Last year's context (FY26)**: $36,318,456 budget, 2.46% expenditure increase, 2.91% tax impact. **Voters approved it in 2025** along with an $86.5M school bond (the third attempt; previous $116M in 2022 and $95M in 2024 were rejected). [WMTW 6/10/25](https://www.wmtw.com/article/cape-elizabeth-voters-approve-865-million-plan-to-build-new-school-renovate-another)
- **2026 status**: As of publication, the Town Council is finalizing its vote on the FY27 budget (May 6, 2026 special meeting). The specific dollar amount, expenditure increase, and tax impact for the FY27 Cape Elizabeth School Department budget should be confirmed once the May 6, 2026 vote is published. Per the February 24, 2026 school board budget workshop: "State Education Subsidy for Cape Elizabeth shows a $162,837.57 decrease" — a notable state funding reduction. Outstanding factors include health premiums, audit results, and two collective bargaining contracts still under negotiation. [Cape Elizabeth Finance]
- **Note on school bond**: The 2025 cycle passed the $86.5M school bond. **No additional school bond referendum is on the June 2026 ballot.** Tax impact of the approved bond would not appear on Cape Elizabeth tax bills until **FY27 / October 2026** per [Advancing Cape Elizabeth Schools](https://www.advancingcape.com/blog/how-much-will-my-taxes-increase-if-the-cape-elizabeth-school-bond-is-approved).
- **No advisory question** on the 2026 ballot as of publication (in 2024 and 2025 the ballot included a non-binding advisory question on the budget being "too high / acceptable / too low" and a continuation vote on the budget validation referendum process; **to be confirmed for 2026 once the warrant is finalized**).

**Historical pattern**: Cape Elizabeth school budget referendums have passed in recent years (2021, 2024, 2025 — all approved by voters). The 2024 vote was 1,570 yes, 634 no. The 2025 vote also passed alongside the school bond. [Cape Elizabeth 2024 results](https://www.capeelizabeth.com/news/post/7788/) · [Cape Elizabeth 2021 results](https://www.capeelizabeth.com/news/post/2560/)
