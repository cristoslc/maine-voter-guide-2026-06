State Representative — Democratic Primaries

You must choose a Democratic primary ballot to vote in these races.

What a State Representative does: One of 151 representatives in Augusta. Votes on every state law and the state budget. Your representative is your closest elected official — the one most likely to answer your call about a local issue. They vote on: the school funding formula, property tax relief programs, minimum wage and labor laws, environmental regulations, housing policy. Two-year term. South Portland is split into three districts.

Context: Retiring Rep. Deqa Dhalac (D-120) asked Jason Shedlock to run for her seat. Dhalac retired citing "persistent racism, harassment and threats" targeting Black and Brown elected officials.

Which district am I in?

Enter your address at the Maine Voter Information Lookup or call City Clerk at (207) 767-3201.

Jason Shedlock — District 120

Democrat
Age 47 · South Portland · Regional Organizer, LIUNA New England · President, Maine State Building & Construction Trades Council (6,000+ workers) · Campaign website

Platform

Labor & Workers
"I come from a long line of blue-collar workers — some with jobs so dangerous it cost them their lives. People with money and power tried to crush my family's hopes and dignity. But we never gave up." Endorsed by outgoing Rep. Deqa Dhalac, who asked him to run.
Campaign website
Affordable Housing
Testified in support of LD 1656 for $100M energy-efficient affordable housing. Wants to address the housing crisis through construction trades workforce development.
Campaign website
Continuing Dhalac's Legacy
Running for the seat vacated by Rep. Deqa Dhalac, who cited "persistent racism, harassment and threats" in deciding not to seek re-election. Dhalac specifically asked Shedlock to run.
WMTW

Background

Regional Organizer for LIUNA New England Organizing Fund. President, Maine State Building & Construction Trades Council (represents 20 affiliates, 6,000+ workers). President, Southern Maine Labor Council. Member, Maine AFL-CIO Executive Board. Member, Maine Climate Council's Buildings & Infrastructure Working Group (gubernatorial appointment). Graduate, Institute for Civic Leadership (now Lift360). Former Special Assistant to Portland Mayor Ethan Strimling. Testified before U.S. Senate Committee on Small Business and Entrepreneurship (2022). From a blue-collar family in Wilkes-Barre, PA.

Endorsements

Maine AFL-CIO (early endorsement, citing 100% pro-labor score). Rep. Deqa Dhalac (asked him to run). Rep. Matthew Beck (HD 122).

Second term · South Portland · IBEW Local 1837 union organizer (retired) · Campaign website · House Democrats page

Background

~20-year career in broadcast journalism (radio, public TV, CBS affiliate) — won awards for news, sports, public affairs, and telethon programming. Then 18 years as IBEW Local 1837 union organizer (2006-2024, retired). Elected March 5, 2024 in special election to succeed the late Rep. Lois Galgay Reckitt. Won full term November 5, 2024, defeating Michael Pock (R). Currently serves on Labor Committee (132nd Legislature). Chaired South Portland Democratic Committee for ~20 years. Wife Denise, three grown children.

Endorsements

Maine AFL-CIO (100% pro-labor score). Sen. Anne Carney. Has endorsed Jason Shedlock for HD 120.

Race Status

Beck is unopposed in the Democratic primary. No Republican filed in District 122. He will be unopposed in November.
