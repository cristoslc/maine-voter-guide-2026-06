US Senate — Republican Primary

This race is uncontested. Susan Collins is the only Republican on the primary ballot. She advances directly to November.

What a US Senate does: Creates federal law. Votes on Supreme Court justices. Approves treaties. Controls federal spending — directing dollars to Maine. Your senator is your voice in Washington on war, taxes, healthcare, Social Security, and civil rights.

Susan Collins

Incumbent Republican
Age 73 · Since 1997 (29 years) · Bangor · Chairs Senate Appropriations Committee (since 2025) · Wikipedia · Ballotpedia

Platform

Trump Relationship
Voted to convict Trump in his second impeachment (one of 7 Republicans). However, was one of 5 Republicans Trump named as deserving to "never be elected again" after voting for the Venezuela War Powers Resolution (January 2026). Described by The Maine Monitor as having "a reputation as a moderate" while being "a key supporter of Trump's Cabinet and judicial nominations."
The Maine Monitor, Jan 8, 2026
Trans Athlete Policy
Backed the petition to place a trans athlete referendum on the 2026 Maine ballot. Collins' office told reporters she "will not be involved in the state referendum campaign because, as a federal official, she does not advocate on state ballot issues."
The Maine Monitor
Venezuela War Powers (Jan 2026)
Voted with Democrats for a War Powers Resolution to curb Trump's ability to expand military operations in Venezuela. Trump responded by naming her among 5 Republicans who "should never be elected to office again." Collins' response: "I guess this means that he would prefer to have Governor Mills or somebody else."
The Maine Monitor, Jan 8, 2026
Impeachment Votes
Voted to acquit Trump in first impeachment (2020), saying Trump "learned a pretty big lesson." Voted to convict in second impeachment (2021) after January 6 — one of seven Republican senators to do so. The Monitor argued she "showed courage" in the second impeachment vote.
The Maine Monitor, Feb 16
2020 Reelection
Won by 8.6 points while Biden won Maine by 9.1 — the last sitting senator to win re-election in a state carried by the opposing party's presidential candidate. In 2026, she faces the same challenge in a midterm with an unpopular Republican president.
NYT, Feb 10, 2026

Record

Collins is Maine's longest-serving member of Congress and the longest-serving Republican woman in Senate history. She chairs the Appropriations Committee — the committee writing every federal spending bill — arguably the most powerful position held by any Mainer in Washington. Her campaign's opening ad highlighted how she secured money for a breakwater dock in Eastport (population ~1,000). The Maine Monitor, Feb 2026

Health

Revealed in early 2026 she has been diagnosed with an essential tremor. Age: 73.

Campaign Message

"I have a proven record of working for you, and I'm running for reelection because my experience, seniority and independence matter." The NRSC calls her "battle-tested." Her argument to voters: keep the Appropriations chair in Maine.

Who You'd Face in November

Note: You vote in only one primary. The Democrat field is shown below so you can evaluate how this primary's candidates match up in the general election.

Graham Platner &amp; David Costello

Democrat
Graham Platner (progressive populist, Marine veteran, endorsed by Bernie Sanders) is the presumptive nominee after Janet Mills dropped out. David Costello (30+ years government experience) remains on the ballot at 3% in polling. Platner leads Collins by 9 points in the latest UNH poll. National Democrats have targeted this race as the top pick-up opportunity for Senate control — they need four flips.
