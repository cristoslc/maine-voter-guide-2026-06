Governor — Democratic Primary

You must choose a Democratic primary ballot to vote in this race.

What a Governor does: Proposes and executes Maine's $10 billion state budget — that's your property taxes, your kid's school funding, your road paving, your healthcare. Appoints hundreds of officials who run state agencies. Signs or vetoes every bill passed by the Legislature. Commands the Maine National Guard. Declares emergencies. Four-year term. Incumbent Janet Mills (D) is term-limited after two consecutive terms.

Context: This primary uses ranked-choice voting with 5 candidates. If no one gets 50% in Round 1, last-place candidate is eliminated and second-choice votes are redistributed until someone hits 50%. Second and third choices matter.

RCV Alliance

Bellows, Jackson, and Pingree announced May 22 they would rank each other as #2 and #3 — explicitly excluding Shah and King. Graham Platner (US Senate candidate) ranks Jackson #1, Bellows #2, Pingree #3. If no candidate hits 50%, the alliance hopes combined votes exceed Shah's in later rounds. Maine Public, May 22

Campaign Finance

King: $795K raised ($700K self-funded); $229K cash.Shah: $234K raised; $288K cash. Pingree: $420K cash on hand (leads Dems). Bellows: $367K cash. Jackson: People's Pledge (no outside money). Outside: 314 Action $650K for Shah. DGA $9M committed for general. BDN, May 30

Background

Born in Wisconsin to Indian immigrant parents. JD+MD from University of Chicago. Worked as economist/epidemiologist at Cambodia Ministry of Health (SARS/avian flu response). Director of Illinois Dept of Public Health (2015-2019). Maine CDC Director (2019-2023) — became household name during COVID daily briefings. Principal Deputy Director of US CDC under Biden (2023-2025). Now professor at Colby College. Highest net favorability (+52) in UNH poll.

Controversies

DFER/314 Action controversy: 314 Action Fund (tied to Democrats for Education Reform, which supports charter schools/school choice) spent $650K on his campaign. Bellows and Jackson issued joint statement: he is "bankrolled by millionaires whose mission is diverting your tax dollars from Maine's neighborhood public schools to private ones." Shah says he does not support vouchers. BDN, May 19. Short Maine residency (~7 years; 2 years at US CDC in Atlanta). As Illinois public health director, opposed medical cannabis and was sued — lost.

Endorsements

314 Action Fund ($650K ad buy). Maine Education Association ranked him #3. No major union or progressive org first-choice endorsements.
