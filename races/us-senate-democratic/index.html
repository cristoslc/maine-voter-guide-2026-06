US Senate — Democratic Primary

You must choose a Democratic primary ballot to vote in this race.

What a US Senate does: Creates federal law with the House. Votes on Supreme Court justices and federal judges. Approves treaties and cabinet officials. Controls federal spending — including directing dollars to Maine for roads, bridges, military installations, and healthcare. One senator can block legislation via holds and filibusters. Six-year term.

Context: Democrats need four Senate flips to retake the chamber. Collins is widely viewed as the most vulnerable Republican incumbent — the only GOP senator running in a state Harris won in 2024.

Graham Platner

Democrat
Age 41 · Sullivan, Maine · Oyster farmer, Marine Corps & Army veteran · First run for office · Campaign website

Platform

Drug Prices
Empower government to negotiate drug costs. "We have politicians of both parties taking Big Pharma checks. I'll put Mainers first, not the drug companies."

Supports across-the-board federal minimum wage increase. Notes NH holds its wage ~$7/hr lower than Maine's.
Campaign platform
Rural Healthcare
"We have rural hospitals closing because of policy choices made by Republicans like Susan Collins. In the Senate, I will fight to fund our rural hospitals."
Campaign platform

Supports Wabanaki self-determination. "Too often, the state and federal governments have run roughshod over tribal sovereignty."
Campaign platform
Iran War
Opposes the 2026 Iran war, calling it a "horrific war of choice." Has said Senator John Fetterman should be voted out for backing it.

Background

First run for office. High school class voted him "most likely to start a revolution." The Maine Monitor profiled him in April 2026, reporting he told his wife the campaign would be "an organizing strategy first and an electoral strategy second."

Former Gov. Janet Mills was recruited into the race by Democratic Senate Leader Chuck Schumer — Platner's victory over her was a significant upset. The Maine Monitor, Feb 7

Endorsed by Bernie Sanders, who called him "a great working class candidate for Senate in Maine who will defeat Susan Collins."

Controversies

Sexual texts (May 30-31, 2026): Wife Amy Gertner told campaign staff in August 2025 she found sexually explicit texts Platner exchanged with multiple women via Kik. Couple attended marriage counseling. Sen. Cory Booker (D-NJ) said Platner has "questions to answer." CNN · NYT

Deleted Reddit comments: The Maine Monitor published the full archive of Platner's since-deleted Reddit posts spanning more than a decade. He deleted his account before launching his Senate bid. Says he sought therapy and has apologized.

Nazi-linked tattoo (2007): Chest tattoo acquired during Marine service. Covered October 2025 — 18 years after getting it. Rep. Auchincloss (D-MA) called it "personally disqualifying." Collins-aligned PACs spent $2M highlighting it. The Hill

Susan Collins

Republican
Age 73 · Since 1997 (29 years) · Chairs Senate Appropriations Committee · Unopposed in Republican primary
Collins is Maine's longest-serving member of Congress. She chairs the committee that writes every federal spending bill — a position that enables her to direct federal dollars to Maine. Her campaign's opening ad highlighted securing money for a breakwater dock in Eastport (pop. ~1,000). The Maine Monitor, Feb 2026

Key votes: Voted to convict Trump in his second impeachment (one of 7 Republicans). Trump later named her among 5 Republicans who "should never be elected again" after she voted for the Venezuela War Powers Resolution (Jan 2026). The Maine Monitor Backed the trans athlete petition for Maine's 2026 ballot. In 2020, won by 8.6 points while Biden carried Maine by 9.1.
