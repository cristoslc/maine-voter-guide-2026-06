## 1. CANDIDATE PROFILES

### Troy Jackson

**Background**: Fifth-generation logger from Allagash (pop. ~23) in Aroostook County. Age 57. AA from University of Maine at Fort Kent. Served in Maine House (2002–2008), then Maine Senate (2008–2014, 2016–2024). Served as Maine Senate President 2018–2024. Initially ran for office as a Republican in 2000 before switching to Democrat. Led 1998 border blockade protesting Canadian loggers working for less. Chaired Senate during COVID. Mills vetoed 40+ of his bills. Ran for ME-02 in 2014, lost primary to Jared Golden's predecessor.

**Endorsements**: Bernie Sanders (I-VT), Rep. Ro Khanna (D-CA), 45+ local labor unions incl. Maine AFL-CIO (first-ever gubernatorial primary endorsement), Maine State Nurses Association/NNU, Maine State Building Trades, Maine Education Association (ranked #2), Sierra Club Maine (co-endorsement), Maine Gun Safety Coalition, Moms Demand Action, Mi'kmaq Tribal Council, Alliance for Retired Americans, 90+ current/former local elected officials, Graham Platner (ranked him #1 for governor). People's Pledge (no outside group money).

**Controversies**: Mills vetoed his glyphosate ban bill (2021). Has been described as campaigning "against" the Mills record at times. His initial Republican registration (2000) has been noted.

**Key quotes**:
- "By the end of my first term, Maine will have free child care for everyone under 125% of median income, we will have a new Department of Affordable Housing, state employees will have a fair contract, public employees will have binding arbitration, and we will have led the nation on prescription drug price reform." ([Maine Public profile](https://www.mainepublic.org/politics/2026-04-17/your-vote-2026-profile-troy-jackson-democrat-for-governor))
- "As far as what you talked about with the streaming tax, I blocked that for six years in a row. As soon as I left, they increased it." (WMTW debate, Apr 30)
- "I think I do disagree with Angus King on the issue of tribal sovereignty. I would make tribal sovereignty a priority in my first days in office." (WMTW debate, Apr 30)

### Nirav Shah

**Background**: Age 49. Born in Wisconsin to Indian immigrant parents. BS University of Louisville; JD and MD from University of Chicago. Worked as economist/epidemiologist at Cambodia Ministry of Health (SARS/avian flu response). Director of Illinois Dept of Public Health (2015–2019). Maine CDC Director (2019–2023) — became household name during COVID daily briefings, "Fans of Dr. Nirav Shah" Facebook page reached 35,000+ members. Principal Deputy Director of US CDC under Biden (2023–2025). NOW a professor at Colby College. Resides in Brunswick.

**Endorsements**: 314 Action Fund ($650K ad buy), Maine Education Association (ranked #3). No major union or progressive org first-choice endorsements.

**Controversies**: (1) Working Families Party attacked him for 314 Action's funding from Democrats for Education Reform (DFER), a group that supports charter schools/school choice — Bellows & Jackson issued joint statement calling him "bankrolled by millionaires whose mission is diverting your tax dollars from Maine's neighborhood public schools to private ones." Shah says he does not support school vouchers. ([Bangor Daily News, May 19](https://www.bangordailynews.com/2026/05/19/politics/elections/maine-nirav-shah-ad-campaign-education-reform-now-advocacy-criticism/)) (2) Short Maine residency (~7 years; 2 years in Atlanta at US CDC). (3) As Illinois public health director, opposed medical cannabis, overrode the advisory committee twice, was sued and lost. ([Sun Journal opinion](https://www.sunjournal.com/2026/05/27/is-a-warm-fuzzy-feeling-enough-to-make-nirav-shah-governor-opinion/)) (4) During COVID, response to needs of minority communities was criticized as slow by some.

**Key quotes**:
- "Mainers want Troy Jackson because Mainers are tired of bullshit." — Campaign mgr Robin Logsdon ([Press Herald, May 27](https://www.pressherald.com/2026/05/27/troy-jackson-surges-in-new-maine-governor-poll/))
- On Day One would issue 6 executive orders including emergency housing mobilization, audit of state-federal data-sharing on immigration, banning masked law enforcement without ID, and requiring body cameras. ([Maine Public, May 29](https://www.mainepublic.org/politics/2026-05-29/nirav-shah-says-he-would-issue-these-six-executive-orders-on-his-first-day-if-elected-governor))
- "I didn't agree with the idea to send $300 checks to everybody in the state." — differing from Mills on rainy day fund use (WMTW debate)

### Shenna Bellows

**Background**: Age 51. Born in Greenfield, MA; grew up in Hancock County, ME. BA Middlebury College. Executive Director of ACLU of Maine (2005–2013). Led Mainers United for Marriage campaign. Executive Director of Holocaust & Human Rights Center of Maine (2018–2020). Maine State Senate (2016–2020) — won a Trump-voting district. Elected Secretary of State by legislature (2021–present) — first woman to hold the role. Ran for US Senate against Susan Collins in 2014, lost by 30+ points.

**Endorsements**: Maine People's Alliance, Sierra Club Maine (co-endorsement), MEA (ranked #1), RCV alliance with Jackson & Pingree. Graham Platner ranked her #2.

**Controversies**: (1) Dec 2023: Ruled Trump ineligible for Maine GOP primary ballot due to Jan 6 — unanimous US Supreme Court overturned. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shenna_Bellows)) (2) May 2026: Invalidated "Protect Girls Sports in Maine" referendum over signature irregularities (forged signatures, out-of-state circulators who didn't follow law); Republicans accused her of political motivation. ([BDN, May 27](https://www.bangordailynews.com/2026/05/26/politics/elections/maine-transgender-athlete-referendum-invalidated/)) (3) DOJ sued her over voter data; Trump-appointed judge dismissed the suit, ruling in her favor. ([maine.gov/sos](https://www.maine.gov/sos/news/federal-court-sides-secretary-bellows-dismisses-doj-lawsuit-over-voter-data))

**Key quotes**:

### Hannah Pingree

Maine House Rep (2002–2010), Speaker of the House (2008–2010) — second woman to hold that role. Chaired North Haven school board. Directed Gov. Mills' Office of Policy Innovation and the Future (GOPIF) 2019–2025, leading climate, housing, opioid response, and New Americans initiatives. Resigned May 16, 2025; announced candidacy June 10, 2025.

**Endorsements**: Gov. Janet Mills (May 19, 2026 — the governor's only endorsement in the race). Maine Conservation Voters Action Fund. Sierra Club Maine (co-endorsement). Several state senators. RCV alliance with Bellows & Jackson.

**Controversies**: (1) Dynasty politics — daughter of sitting US Rep Chellie Pingree; critics note family advantage. (2) Close association with Mills administration — Jackson has effectively campaigned against Mills' record at times, creating tension. (3) On tribal sovereignty, acknowledged she didn't push Mills on the issue: "On tribal sovereignty, that was not in my portfolio. I really didn't talk to her about those issues." ([PBS interview](https://www.pbs.org/video/maine-democratic-gubernatorial-interviews-zjsen1/))

**Key quotes**:

([Maine Public profile](https://www.mainepublic.org/politics/2026-04-17/your-vote-2026-profile-hannah-pingree-democrat-for-governor))

- On alliance: "I think we're the three people in this race who have voted time and time again on behalf of Maine people, on behalf of working people, on behalf of universal health care, on behalf of the environment, on behalf of more housing for Maine people." ([Maine Public, May 26](https://www.mainepublic.org/politics/2026-05-26/jackson-bellows-pingree-will-rank-each-other-while-shah-leads-democratic-gubernatorial-primary))

Lives in Portland with wife Catherine Alexandre and two children. Private school education (Camden Hills). Moderate/centrist positioning. Running as outsider despite political dynasty.

**Endorsements**: No major organizational endorsements. No RCV alliance. Father Sen. Angus King Jr. has appeared in campaign ads. Has been endorsed by some local officials.

**Controversies**: (1) Running on name recognition / family dynasty — Press Herald profile headlined "running on his resume, not his name" but acknowledged the name is the resume. ([Maine Public, Mar 20](https://www.mainepublic.org/politics/2026-03-20/maine-gubernatorial-candidates-mostly-back-tribal-sovereignty-in-wabanaki-hosted-forum)) (4) Only candidate not committed to universal healthcare or universal childcare. (5) Self-funded $700K of $795K raised in latest period.

**Key quotes**:

## 2. PLATFORM POSITIONS BY CANDIDATE

### Healthcare / MaineCare

| Candidate | Position |
|-----------|----------|
| **Jackson** | Supports Medicare for All. Would take on Big Pharma on prescription drug prices (~50% of costs in ME). Sponsored Rx price bills vetoed by Mills. Wants to expand MaineCare and public health infrastructure. |
| **Shah** | Cap deductibles, copays, out-of-pocket costs. End prior authorization delays. Give state power to negotiate Rx prices. Fund new residency positions and expand loan repayment for healthcare workers. Day 1 EO on protecting reproductive rights. |
| **Bellows** | Universal healthcare as top priority. $400M for universal childcare. Expand rural healthcare access. Fight for reproductive and maternity care against Trump admin cuts. |
| **Pingree** | Take on big insurance companies, fight for public option. Invest in healthcare workforce. Shore up public health safety net. Protect reproductive and maternity care. |
| **King** | Support innovations that lower drug prices. Allow doctors to focus on patient care not paperwork. Better rural health solutions. Expand training for healthcare workers including HS exposure. Invest in preventative care. More moderate — has NOT committed to universal healthcare. |

### Housing

| Candidate | Position |
|-----------|----------|
| **Jackson** | Create Department of Housing Affordability. Accelerate new construction. Ban private equity from buying mobile home parks. Assist homeowners/renters with essential repairs. Invest in manufactured housing. Statewide housing appeals board to cut red tape. Tax credits for renters. Support UMaine Orono 3D-printed wood fiber homes. |
| **Shah** | Emergency Mobilization on Housing (Day 1 EO): single housing coordinator, inventory state land/buildings for homes, cut permitting delays, protect affordable housing, public dashboard tracking progress toward 84,000-home goal. First-time homebuyer mortgage guarantee via MaineHousing. Infrastructure fund for water/sewer to enable new developments. |
| **Bellows** | Expand housing fund for construction & rehabilitation. Create training model for trades via apprenticeship programs. Boost first-time homebuyers program. Invest in eviction prevention and security deposit assistance. Housing-first approach. Support recovery homes. Tenant bill of rights. Property tax freeze for residents funded by taxes on out-of-state homeowners. |
| **Pingree** | Invest $100M/year in affordable housing development and preservation. Take on out-of-state corporations and private equity driving up rents. Cut red tape and streamline permitting. End veteran homelessness in first year. Reduce evictions. Expand permanent housing solutions for most vulnerable. |
| **King** | Support towns adding housing and weatherization for aging homes. Remove red tape slowing progress. Focus on training more building trades workers. Protect mobile home owners. Proactively house those most in need. |

### Taxes / Property Tax

| Candidate | Position |
|-----------|----------|
| **Jackson** | Make working families and seniors, not the wealthy, get the tax break. Supports the 2% millionaire's surcharge. Free childcare for families under 125% of median income. Blocked streaming tax for 6 years in Senate. |
| **Shah** | Supports millionaire's tax (2% surcharge on income above $1M). Focus on lowering overall costs for Mainers. First-time homebuyer program. Property tax relief via efficiency and smarter development. |
| **Bellows** | Economic New Deal for Maine: freeze property taxes for residents + double taxes on non-residents + 4% millionaires tax generating $200M+/year for healthcare, housing, childcare, education. Filed for statewide property tax freeze. |
| **Pingree** | First to propose tourist tax on short-term rentals. Support millionaire's surcharge. Real property tax relief for working families. Take on out-of-state corporations. |
| **King** | Use millionaire's surcharge revenue for middle-class relief. Opposed Mills' $300 rebate from rainy day fund — said fund needed for future budget gaps. More cautious on spending commitments. |

### Energy / Climate

| Candidate | Position |
|-----------|----------|
| **Jackson** | Ask PUC to stop hiking electricity rates. Convene energy task force for solutions. Ban aerial herbicide spraying (glyphosate) — Mills vetoed his 2021 bill. Advance clean energy while protecting affordability. Conservation plan released May 29. |
| **Shah** | Crack down on unnecessary utility spending. Expand energy assistance and efficiency programs. Work with regional partners to prevent winter price spikes. Build modern reliable energy system — upgrade grid, accelerate low-cost homegrown energy like wind. Protect ratepayers from AI data center cost increases. |
| **Bellows** | Cap utility company rate of return at 6%. Streamline permitting/procurement for clean energy projects. Solar panel installation for LIHEAP-eligible homes. Advance clean energy while tackling affordability and climate goals. |
| **Pingree** | Maine Conservation Voters Action Fund — cited her "unmatched record" on climate. As legislator: passed first-in-nation Kid's Safe Products Act, led ME into RGGI. As GOPIF director: led climate council, 4-year climate plan. Protect ratepayers from AI data centers. Advance tribal sovereignty collaboration on climate. |
| **King** | Private-sector renewable energy background (Peaks Renewables). Focus on permitting and interconnection reform — byzantine processes add cost and delay. Generate solar at 3x lower cost than current approach. Use renewable energy to decrease power costs. More moderate pace — not committed to sweeping renewables targets. |

### Tribal Sovereignty

| Candidate | Position |
|-----------|----------|
| **Jackson** | Strong supporter. Has legislative record — sponsored bills to restore Wabanaki sovereignty, Mills vetoed it. Endorsed by Mi'kmaq Tribal Council. Said would make it "a priority in my first days in office." Debated disagreement with King. |
| **Shah** | Supports restoration. Suggested speed would be a factor. Would invite Wabanaki leaders into administration. |
| **Bellows** | Strong supporter. Day 1 priority. Will invite Wabanaki leaders to serve in administration. Would formalize government-to-government partnerships. |
| **Pingree** | Supports full restoration. Worked with tribal representatives through climate council on government-to-government interaction. Acknowledged she didn't push Mills on sovereignty. "That was not in my portfolio." |
| **King** | HEDGED. Said he needs to "better understand the issue" before making guarantees. Only Democrat not firmly committed. This was a notable debate disagreement. |

### Immigration

| Candidate | Position |
|-----------|----------|
| **Jackson** | Would pursue lawsuit against ICE for illegal searches/seizures under 4th Amendment. As founding member of Maine Immigrants' Rights Coalition, committed to supporting New Mainers. Stand up for rights regardless of status. Took strongest anti-ICE stance — said law enforcement violating constitutional rights should be arrested. |
| **Shah** | Six Day 1 EOs including: audit state-federal data sharing on immigration, direct AG to take legal action against federal agents who violate law/Constitution, push legislation banning masked law enforcement and requiring ID and body cameras. "Next ICE surge is a distinct possibility." |
| **Bellows** | Will not allow state collaboration with Trump admin on immigration enforcement. Provide legal protections in churches, schools, hospitals, courthouses as safe spaces. Require agents to unmask and clearly identify themselves. As SOS, refused to give voter data to federal government — won in court. |
| **Pingree** | Through GOPIF, helped start Office of New Americans. Will stand up against Trump admin overreach on immigration. Differed from Mills on ICE cooperation — disagreed with Mills' delay in signing bill limiting state cooperation with ICE. |
| **King** | Less specific on this issue in debates. More moderate posture overall. |

### Abortion / Reproductive Rights

All five candidates strongly support reproductive rights. No significant daylight in positions. All would protect access and oppose Trump admin restrictions. Key distinction: **Bellows** and **Pingree** emphasize this as a top-tier issue; **Shah** included it in Day 1 executive orders; **Jackson** and **King** less vocal on this specific issue.

### Education

| **Shah** | $60M plan to cut child food insecurity from 20% to 10%. Expand school meal programs. Streamline bureaucracy. MEA ranked him #3. 314 Action (tied to DFER/school choice group) spent $650K on his behalf — rivals criticized this. Shah says he doesn't support vouchers. |
| **Bellows** | Universal childcare ($400M estimate). Oppose voucher schemes. MEA ranked #1. "We agree that voucher schemes that drain funding from our public schools have no place in Maine." |

## 4. RCV ALLIANCE DETAILS

**Announced**: May 22, 2026, at a joint press conference in Portland. Reiterated May 26, 2026 at a second event with Graham Platner.

**Members**: Troy Jackson, Shenna Bellows, Hannah Pingree

**Structure**: Each asked supporters to rank the other two as #2 and #3. Explicitly excluded both Shah and King from their rankings.

**Quotes**:
- Jackson: "I've worked with @shennabellows and @PingreeHannah for a long time, and I respect the hell out of them both. I am proud to rank them on my ballot." (Twitter, May 22)
- Pingree: "I've known Troy and Shenna since 2002 — we grew up together in Maine politics and have stood on the front lines of some of the most important fights this state has seen." (Statement, May 22)
- Bellows: "We also agree that voucher schemes that drain funding from our public schools have no place in Maine." ([Maine AFL-CIO](https://maineaflcio.org/news/jackson-bellows-pingree-will-rank-each-other-gubernatorial-primary))

**Graham Platner's ranking**: Jackson (#1) → Bellows (#2) → Pingree (#3). Announced May 26 at Portland event.

**Context**: This followed a Sierra Club joint endorsement of all three on April 22 in Hallowell. MEA ranked Bellows (#1), Jackson (#2), Shah (#3). MSEA ranked Jackson (#1), Bellows (#2).

**Strategic rationale**: The alliance aims to consolidate progressive/institutional voters. If no candidate hits 50% in RCV round 1, the alliance hopes that combined, their votes exceed Shah's. Pan Atlantic found first+second preferences: Shah 39%, King 33%, Pingree 32%, Bellows 31%, Jackson 18%. The alliance's math depends on RCV eliminating King and then their voters redistributing.

Sources: [Maine Public, May 22](https://www.mainepublic.org/politics/2026-05-22/shenna-bellows-troy-jackson-and-hannah-pingree-form-ranked-choice-alliance-in-gubernatorial-primary); [Portland Press Herald, May 22](https://www.pressherald.com/2026/05/22/these-3-maine-governor-candidates-are-forming-an-alliance/); [FairVote](https://fairvote.org/how-ranked-choice-voting-is-impacting-the-maine-primary/)

## 6. KEY DEBATE MOMENTS / DISAGREEMENTS

### First televised debate (CBS13/BDN, May 5)
- **Tribal sovereignty**: Jackson explicitly disagreed with King's hedging: "I think I do disagree with Angus King on the issue of tribal sovereignty. I would make tribal sovereignty a priority in my first days in office."
- **Property taxes**: Bellows claimed priority on property tax freeze; Pingree noted she'd worked on housing/property taxes since the early 2000s.
- **Streaming tax**: Jackson reminded audience he blocked the streaming tax for six years in the Senate.
- **ICE/immigration**: All five decried ICE tactics. Jackson went furthest — said law enforcement violating constitutional rights should be arrested. Shah outlined 6 Day 1 executive orders. Pingree differed from Mills on ICE cooperation.
- **Rainy day fund**: King opposed Mills' $300 rebate checks; others were more supportive of the concept.

### Final debate (ME State Chamber/NCM, May 14)
- **Housing**: Pingree proposed $100M/year housing production. Shah proposed an infrastructure fund for water/sewer. Bellows proposed $400M for universal childcare (the biggest ticket item). Jackson said the childcare estimate should be closer to $300M since Maine already spends $60M.
- **School choice / vouchers**: Bellows and Jackson criticized Shah for 314 Action's ties to DFER (pro-school-choice). Shah says he opposes vouchers.
- **King as moderate outlier**: Not committed to universal healthcare, universal childcare, or tribal sovereignty as Day 1 priority.
- **Shah vs. status quo**: Shah positioned himself as outsider challenging the system. Alliance trio countered with their legislative records.

### Renters forum (USM, May 29)
- **Jackson**: "[Private equity] is coming in here, buying these places up and forcing people out by raising rates, and the greed of this industry is insatiable." Supports tax credits for renters, 3D-printed homes.
- **Pingree**: Pledged to end veteran homelessness in first year.
- **Bellows**: Housing-first approach, recovery homes, tenant bill of rights.
- **Shah**: Housing emergency mobilization on Day 1.
- **King**: No specific renter proposal; focused on housing for workers/employers.

Sources: [WMTW debate](https://www.wmtw.com/article/debate-maine-governor-democratic-primary-live-updates/71174569); [Bangor Daily News debate coverage](https://www.bangordailynews.com/2026/04/30/politics/maine-democrats-1st-tv-gubernatorial-debate/); [Maine Morning Star debate recap](https://mainemorningstar.com/2026/05/14/major-takeaways-from-the-democratic-gubernatorial-candidates-final-debate/); [BDN renters forum](https://www.bangordailynews.com/2026/05/29/politics/elections/democrats-maine-governor-renters/)
