Research on best nonpartisan voter guide exemplars to extract a canonical data model.

## Key Findings

1. **LWV/VOTE411 model**: Candidates answer open-ended questions, responses published verbatim. Strongest nonpartisanship guardrails.
2. **Guides.vote model**: Journalists write position summaries with source citations. Includes candidate quiz feature.
3. **Ballotpedia model**: Professional researchers compile from multiple sources. Most comprehensive federal/state coverage but limited locally.
4. **BallotReady model**: Aggregated from candidate websites, social media, press, endorsers. Research completed ~30 days before election.
5. **The Maine Monitor model**: Investigative journalism approach with campaign finance data integration. State-level only.

## Gaps Identified

- No exemplar provides South Portland city-level coverage specifically
- School board races are poorly covered in national guides
- Local ballot questions lack pro/con depth outside LWV coverage
- Candidate non-response is a universal problem
