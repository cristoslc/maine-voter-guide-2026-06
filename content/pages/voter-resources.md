## How to Register

- **Online**: [registertovote.sos.maine.gov](https://registertovote.sos.maine.gov/) (deadline: May 19 by 5 PM)
- **By mail**: Download and mail a voter registration card (deadline: May 19)
- **In person**: At the South Portland City Clerk's office, 25 Cottage Road, during business hours — or at your polling place on election day
- **BMV**: Register through any Bureau of Motor Vehicles branch (deadline: June 2)

**ID**: First-time registrants need to show proof of residence. Acceptable documents include a Maine driver's license, utility bill, bank statement, or government document with name and address.

## Check Your Registration

- Call the City Clerk: (207) 767-3201
- Check online: [Voter Information Lookup](https://www.maine.gov/portal/government/edemocracy/voter_lookup.php)

## Semi-Open Primary

Maine holds semi-open primaries (since March 5, 2024):

- **Unenrolled** (not registered with any party) voters may choose to vote in one party's primary
- **Enrolled** voters will receive their party's ballot
- You may change party enrollment up to 15 days before the election

## Absentee Voting

You do not need an excuse to vote absentee in Maine (no-excuse absentee voting). Ballots are available 30 days before the election.

- **Request online**: [absenteeballotrequest.sos.maine.gov](https://absenteeballotrequest.sos.maine.gov)
- **Phone**: Call the City Clerk at (207) 767-3201
- **In person**: Vote absentee at City Hall starting May 11
- **Deadline** to request: **Thursday, June 4 at 5:00 PM** (unless special circumstances — see below)
- **Return** by 8 PM on June 9 — use the secure drop box at the rear of City Hall, mail it in, or return to the City Clerk's office. Allow at least **7 days for postal delivery** if mailing.
- **Track your ballot**: [apps.web.maine.gov/cgi-bin/online/AbsenteeBallot/ballot_status.pl](https://apps.web.maine.gov/cgi-bin/online/AbsenteeBallot/ballot_status.pl)

### Ongoing Absentee Ballot Program

You can enroll to automatically receive absentee ballots by mail for future elections — no need to re-request each time. [Download the enrollment form](https://www.maine.gov/sos/sites/maine.gov.sos/files/inline-files/3%20Ongoing%20AbsenteeStatusApp%20fillable%20pdf%20FINAL.pdf) and return to the City Clerk.

### After the Deadline — Special Circumstances Absentee

If you miss the June 4 deadline, you can still request an absentee ballot if you meet one of four exceptions:
- Unexpected absence from South Portland during all polling hours on Election Day
- Physical disability
- Incapacity or illness preventing you from leaving home or a treatment facility
- Coastal island resident unable to travel to the polls

### Accessible Voting

Voters with print disabilities can use the **Electronic Accessible Absentee Ballot** to vote privately and independently. [Learn more on the Secretary of State's website](https://www.maine.gov/sos/elections-voting/accessible-voting).

## Find Your Polling Place

South Portland has multiple polling locations. Find yours at: [southportland.gov/586/Polling-Locations](https://southportland.gov/586/Polling-Locations)

**Important change**: Unlike the June 2025 school budget election (which was consolidated at the Community Center), for June 9, 2026, **all polling places areopen**. Vote at your regular polling location (the one where you vote in November elections).
